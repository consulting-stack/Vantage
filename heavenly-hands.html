Our Commitment to You — and the Law

Heavenly Hands operates in full compliance with every federal and state law governing the credit repair industry. This is not a policy we revisit — it is the foundation our business is built on.

We are bound by and strictly adhere to the following regulatory frameworks:

Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) — 15 U.S.C. §§ 1679–1679j

Enforced by the Federal Trade Commission, CROA governs every credit repair organization in the United States. Under this law, Heavenly Hands will never accept payment before services have been fully rendered, will always provide a written contract prior to performing any service, and will always honor your right to cancel that contract within three (3) business days of signing — without penalty, without obligation, and without question.

Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) — 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.

The FCRA gives consumers the right to dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information on their credit reports. Heavenly Hands operates exclusively within these boundaries. We do not dispute accurate information, we do not fabricate pretexts, and we do not make representations about your credit file that are false. Every dispute letter we generate is grounded in a legitimate, documentable basis.

Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR) — 16 C.F.R. Part 310

Enforced jointly by the FTC and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), the TSR prohibits credit repair organizations from requesting or collecting advance fees for services not yet performed. Heavenly Hands complies unconditionally. We do not solicit by deceptive means, we do not misrepresent outcomes, and we do not guarantee specific results — because no legitimate credit repair organization can.

Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) — 15 U.S.C. §§ 6801–6827

As an organization handling nonpublic personal financial information, Heavenly Hands is subject to the GLBA's Privacy Rule and Safeguards Rule. Your data is collected only for the purpose of providing the services you have requested. It is never sold, never shared with unaffiliated third parties without your consent, and is protected under a documented information security program.

FTC Act — Section 5 (15 U.S.C. § 45)

The Federal Trade Commission's broad authority to prohibit unfair or deceptive acts or practices applies to every consumer-facing representation we make. Our marketing, our pricing, our contracts, and our communications are written to inform — not to mislead.

We only work under written contracts that include the full cancellation period required by law. No verbal agreements. No informal arrangements. Every client engagement begins with a dated, signed contract that clearly states the services to be performed, the total cost, the payment schedule, and your unconditional right to cancel within three business days.

If you believe your rights under any of these laws have been violated by any credit repair organization — including Heavenly Hands — you have the right to file a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission at ReportFraud.ftc.gov, or contact your state's Attorney General office.

This section is provided for informational purposes. It does not constitute legal advice. Heavenly Hands recommends that all consumers review their rights independently.
